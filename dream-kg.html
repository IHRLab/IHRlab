Proto-OKN Theme 1: DREAM-KG

Develop Dynamic, REsponsive, Adaptive, and Multifaceted Knowledge Graphs to address homelessness with Explainable AI

Principal Investigator: Huanmei Wu, PhD

Co-Investigators: Chiu Tan, PhD; Yuzhou Chen, PhD; Ying Ding, PhD; Philip McCallion, PhD; Omar Martinez, JD, MPH, MS

Sponsor: National Science Foundation

Timeline: 10/01/2023 – 09/30/2026

Project Overview

The Dynamic, Responsive, Engaged, Adaptive, and Multifaceted Knowledge Graph (DREAM-KG) project is building a knowledge graph to support homelessness triage, service coordination, and data integration across sectors. This innovative initiative develops a comprehensive knowledge graph that will provide essential insights into the social, economic, and political factors contributing to homelessness while triaging existing resources.

Introduction

This project is developing a knowledge graph that will provide a comprehensive understanding of the social, economic, and political factors that contribute to homelessness while triaging existing resources. Developed in partnership with a Community Advisory Board (CAB) composed of social service providers, clinicians, nonprofit leaders, policy advocates, and individuals with lived experience, DREAM-KG leverages community expertise to ensure usability, equity, and ethical implementation.

The knowledge graph will equip users with essential insights to address the homelessness problem more effectively. This includes understanding:

  • Core structural factors contributing to homelessness
  • Effective intervention methods and evidence-based practices
  • Community resources and service availability
  • Cultural nuances across diverse backgrounds
  • Policy knowledge from local to federal levels

Impact & Goals

DREAM-KG aims to revolutionize how communities approach homelessness by:

  • Creating an integrated, searchable knowledge base that connects datasets and models across multiple sectors
  • Enabling better service coordination and resource allocation through data integration
  • Supporting evidence-based decision making for policy makers, service providers, and community organizations
  • Ensuring equity and ethical implementation through community-centered co-design
  • Providing explainable AI insights that build trust and transparency

Community Partnership

A key strength of DREAM-KG is its collaborative approach through a Community Advisory Board composed of diverse stakeholders including social service providers, clinicians, nonprofit leaders, policy advocates, and individuals with lived experience of homelessness. This partnership ensures the knowledge graph addresses real community needs and maintains a commitment to equity and social justice.
