DT4PCP: A Digital Twin Framework for Personalized Care Planning Applied to Type 2 Diabetes Management
Javad Mohammad Alizadeh, Mukesh Kumar Patel, Huanmei Wu (2025).

Digital Twin (DT) technology has emerged as a transformative approach in healthcare, but its application in personalized patient care remains limited. We introduce a general DT framework for personalized care planning (DT4PCP), with the core components being a real-time virtual representation of a patient's health and emerging predictive models to enable adaptive, personalized care. We implemented the DT4PCP framework for managing Type 2 Diabetes (DT4PCP-T2D), enabling real-time collection of behavioral data from patients with T2D, predicting emergency department (ED) risks, simulating the effects of different interventions, and personalizing care strategies to reduce ED visits.

Predicting Emergency Department Visits for Patients with Type II Diabetes
Javad M Alizadeh, Jay S Patel, Gabriel Tajeu, Yuzhou Chen, Ilene L Hollin, Mukesh K Patel, Junchao Fei, Huanmei Wu (2024).

Over 30 million Americans are affected by Type II diabetes (T2D), a treatable condition with significant health risks. This study aims to develop and validate predictive models using machine learning (ML) techniques to estimate emergency department (ED) visits among patients with T2D. Ensemble Learning and Random Forest models demonstrated superior predictive performance in terms of discrimination, calibration, and clinical applicability.

The Current Landscape of Cybersecurity Training in CAHIIM Accredited Programs
Huanmei Wu, Mukesh Kumar Patel, Chiu C Tan (2024).

Cybersecurity training for health professionals is important for different stakeholders in healthcare. This project aims to shed light on the current landscapes of cybersecurity training in CAHIIM-accredited programs in Health information management (HIM) and health informatics using natural language processing (NLP). The research identifies trends in cybersecurity education within healthcare, emphasizing the integration of privacy and security measures in academic programs.

The Landscape of Health Informatics Education in Low-or Middle-Income Countries
Jay S Patel, Omotese Oaikhena, Thanh TD Phan, Hoa Vo, Javad M Alizadeh, Huanmei Wu (2023).

Health informatics education is an essential part of global health. However, the available health informatics programs across the globe are unequal. This study aims to identify the current state of health informatics education in Low- or Middle-Income Countries, focusing on Africa and Southeast Asia. We found most health informatics related education only exist in the USA, while in the other countries little to no education exists related to health informatics.
